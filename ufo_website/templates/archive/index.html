Traversing Through the Data: UFO Sightings


In pop culture, the term “UFO” or Unidentified Flying Object – has become an aerial phenomenon. Leading back to the 1950’s, the US Air Force served as liaison to vet all sightings whereas later forming the UFOB – signifying any airborne object with aerodynamic features or unusual characters that do not conform to our aircrafts or missile types. Conversely, the sighting of UFOs date back as early as 240 BC and possibly as early as 467 BC. – later record as Halley’s Comet. Historically, UFO searchers linked sightings to more conventional religious images; medieval paintings; and various forms of bright meteors.

The late 1940s & 1950s referred to the UFOs as “flying saucers” or “flying discs” which garnered considerable interest during the Cold War. This era was particularly sensitive due to the heightened concern for national security.

UFOs have been a common theme of study over the years with a wide range of scope and scientific rigor. Governments or independent academics in the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, and several other countries are known to have investigated UFO reports. Amongst the most preeminent known governmental study was the ghost rockets investigation by the Swedish military (1946–1947), another was Project Blue Book, previously Project Sign and Project Grudge, led by the USAF from 1947 until 1969, the secret U.S. Army/Air Force Project Twinkle investigation into green fireballs sightings (1948–1951).
