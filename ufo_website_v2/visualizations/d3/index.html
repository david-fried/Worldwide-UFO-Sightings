Lower Incomes Correlate with Higher Rates of Obesity, Smoking, and Inadequate Healthcare

The lower one's income, the higher one's likelihood of poor health. Data shows that individuals with lower incomes have higher rates of obesity and nicotine consumption. Although Americans with lower income lack healthy habits more often, they also have less access to the healthcare they need.
